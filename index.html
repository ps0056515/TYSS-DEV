THE PROBLEM

15 years of expertise.
Zero infrastructure
to hold it.

Training organisations carry enormous domain knowledge — and have no system to capture, structure, or scale it with AI.

01
Knowledge disappears with people
When a senior trainer leaves, their years of practical insight — the analogies, the edge cases, the interview patterns — walk out with them. There is no system to capture it.
02
Generic AI gives generic answers
ChatGPT doesn't know QSpiders curriculum, style, or standards. It gives the same answer to every student regardless of their course, level, or what company they're interviewing with.
03
Content creation doesn't compound
The same concepts get re-explained by different trainers thousands of times. No institutional memory. No compounding value. The organisation is always starting from scratch.

HOW IT CONNECTS

From raw knowledge
to intelligent
delivery.

Four platforms. One continuous loop. Each step feeds the next. The system compounds over time.

01
SABER captures everything.
15 years of testing expertise — trainer sessions, PDFs, student Q&A, WhatsApp threads, lab exercises — ingested, structured into training pairs, and used to fine-tune a domain LLM on E2E GPU infrastructure. The knowledge stops living in people and starts living in the system.
SABER → L1 TO L7 PIPELINE
02
SkillForge amplifies trainers.
Trainers stop writing content from scratch. SkillForge generates lecture notes, lab exercises, assessments, and slide decks in QSpiders style — and gives trainers AI tools to stay current with industry shifts. The trainer's role shifts from content creator to content curator.
SKILLFORGE → CONTENT GENERATION
03
QANTAM serves students.
Students interact with a QSpiders-trained AI, not a generic chatbot. Doubts resolved 24/7. Learning paths personalised to individual gaps. Mock interviews calibrated to the exact companies QSpiders students interview at — TCS, Infosys, Wipro, product startups.
QANTAM → STUDENT INTELLIGENCE
04
Every interaction improves everything.
Student questions, trainer corrections, placement outcomes — all flow back into SABER as enriched training data. The model retrains automatically when thresholds are hit. Year 2 is measurably smarter than Year 1. The knowledge compounds indefinitely.
FEEDBACK LOOP → AUTO RETRAIN

QSpiders is the reference implementation. The first organisation to run the full SABER pipeline. Everything we build here — the ingestion connectors, the training orchestrator, the serving layer — is designed for every organisation that follows.

The knowledge infrastructure problem isn't unique to QSpiders. It belongs to every training company, every corporate L&D team, every university department that has deep domain expertise and no system to scale it with AI.

We're not building AI tools. We're building the layer that makes domain-specific AI possible for organisations that don't have ML teams.
